Analysis of 1984 by George Orwell

George Orwell's 1984 is a dystopian novel that explores themes of totalitarianism, censorship, and individuality.

Summary

The story follows Winston Smith, a member of the Outer Party, who works at the Ministry of Truth, where he alters historical records to fit the narrative of the Party.

Key Themes

  • Totalitarianism
  • Censorship
  • Societal control
  • Individuality versus collectivism

Conclusion

Orwell's message about the dangers of oppressive governments remains relevant today.
